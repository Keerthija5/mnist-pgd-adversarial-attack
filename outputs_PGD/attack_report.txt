CNN Robustness Evaluation under PGD-L2 Adversarial Attacks

Dataset: MNIST
Training samples used: 60000
Test samples used: 10000
Attacked subset size: 200
Clean test accuracy: 0.9834
Clean attacked-subset accuracy: 1.0000

Attack setup:
- Attack: PGD-L2
- max_iter: 40
- eta: 0.2
- dmax sweep: [0.5, 1.0, 1.5, 2.0, 2.5, 3.0]

Main result:
- At the strongest attack budget dmax=3.0, adversarial accuracy dropped to 0.2100.
- Accuracy drop at strongest attack: 0.7900.
- Attack success rate at strongest attack: 0.7900.
- Average L2 perturbation norm at strongest attack: 2.7595.

Interpretation:
Clean accuracy alone is not enough to judge model reliability. The same CNN that performs well on clean MNIST images becomes much less reliable when small adversarial perturbations are introduced.
The model first falls below the 50% reliability threshold at dmax=2.5.
